Julian comes to UCSF by way of Lewis & Clark College, where he received his BA in Chemistry. While at Lewis & Clark College, Julian worked in the lab of Casey Jones to design and engineer polyphenol releasing coronary arterial stents. Julian is a graduate student in the Chemistry & Chemical Biology program at UCSF. In the Manglik lab, Julian is studying mechanisms of GPCR-coupled activation with support from the ​National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship.
